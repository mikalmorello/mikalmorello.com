Tufts Magazine

Tasked with the challenge of bringing an award-winning print publication to life online, we had to come up with a dynamic web platform to support the multimedia storytelling needs of Tufts Magazine
Project Role
  • Front-end Web Developer

Living primarily as a print publication up until this point, the magazine had to be rethought, both in terms of how it was to be presented and curated. The magazine could no longer be merely translated to the web, but designed for the web. As part of the “Digital First” content strategy, Tufts Magazine was leading the way on how Tufts would approach digital storytelling. The magazine would include highly curated content in the form of photo essays, galleries, videos, and interactive features.

The magazine’s concept designs were both beautiful and deceivingly simple. Beneath the pixel perfect layouts was a need for a complex data structure and a flexible presentation layer. We relied heavily upon a robust tagging system and issue based architecture to feed content throughout the site. Users would be able to explore content by issue, related categories, or through curated topics. Weaving in news and web only features would keep the content fresh between issues.

The website empowered editors to customize the look and feel of their content, and control how it would be presented. As if thumbing through a magazine, the article layouts should look visually interesting and distinct from one another. To this end, editors would be able to select from various article templates, layouts, themes, and treatments. Building articles ranging from long form immersive stories to quick read lists.

As developers, we had to balance the complexity of the editing experience against the flexibility of the article’s design. Many of the dynamic design features were hidden behind simple pre-configured options.

Digitally Crafted

Article designs are carefully crafted for both online and print publications.

The design team went as far as to create unique banner designs for each screen breakpoint. A challenge at the time, we were able to develop the system to allow editors to manage the focal point, aspect ratio, and pixel density of each banner image, based upon the rendered screen size. This allowed us to offer an optimized user experience, regardless of the device or platform the site was viewed on.

Editor Driven

The layout of an article's banner, headline, and lead can be easily changed through a simple select dropdown in the admin interface.

In this build, we experienced both the power and shortcomings of being an early Drupal 8 adopter. The tools available to us did not always feel fully baked, but with a little ingenuity we were able to develop a flexible system that continues to grow and support the evolving needs of the magazine today.

Mobile Optimized

With 70% of our web traffic coming from mobile devices, it was important to curate an engaging mobile experience.
